Video 2: 15 Minute Daily Habit to Make You Millions! by Chris Voss & The Black Swan Group

學習戰術性同理心 (Tactical Empathy)、標籤化 (Labeling) 和校準式提問 (Calibrated Questions) 的微練習。

影片逐字稿 (Transcript)

Because you want to become a Jedi in the room. The Jedi is the person who never gets steamrololled, always ends up getting heard, and seems to know exactly what to say when the pressure is on without being loud is the truth. That person isn't born, they're built. 15 minutes a day is all it takes. Not three hours, not a week-l long retreat, just a daily 15-minute negotiation habit. Let me show you what it does.

Most people go into big conversations cold, like walking into a ring without warming up. They read the book once, maybe watched a master class, but then they expect their brain to perform under pressure. That's not how skill works. Negotiation, just like jujitsu or jazz piano, is neural. You don't know it, you become it. And just like you wouldn't bench 300 lbs without training, you can't walk into a raise conversation or a boardroom battle and expect tactical empathy to roll off your tongue without practice.

Here's what 15 minutes a day can look like. For three minutes, label the headlines. Grab a news story or trending topic. Practice three labels based on the emotional dynamics. Say this stuff out loud. It sounds like the public's frustrated. It looks like the company's scrambling. It seems like people feel betrayed. Then three minutes of calibrated question practice or repetitions, reps as we call them. Choose a tough situation and ask, "What makes that difficult? How would you like it to look instead? What's the biggest risk if this doesn't change?" Again, you got to say these out loud.

Next three minutes. Mirror out loud. Take a conversation you had that day. Play it back in your head. Now imagine yourself mirroring their last three to five words and imagine yourself doing it with a tone of curiosity. Notice how I did that. Also notice there's some body language that helps. I tilt my head to the side, lifted my head up a little bit. That'll help you get that tone right.

The three minutes next for the accusations audit warm-up. Accusations audit is a list of the negative things that people could be saying. Could be, not have, could be. Think of a future conversation. What are you afraid they're going to say? Say it before they do. This is probably going to sound selfish. There's a pretty good chance you're afraid they're going to think you're selfish. Call it out. This is probably going to sound selfish. It's a pretty good chance, depending upon the circumstances, that they're going to think you're trying to dodge responsibility. So, you say, "You might think I'm just trying to dodge responsibility. You might think this is going to be harsh." Anticipation. Practice it out loud.

Next three minutes, play back a real conversation and journal for three minutes. What did I miss? What could I have labeled? What didn't I hear? That's it. No scripts, no memorizing, just micro reps.

Let's talk about how this compounds. Day one, you'll feel awkward. Day five, you'll start noticing things in conversations other people miss. Day 15, your tone changes. You stop reacting. You listen more. After 30 days, here's what you get. Fluency and tactical empathy. You'll stop fumbling. Labeling becomes instinct. Emotional control. You hear an attack, you stay calm. You label the threat instead of taking it personally. Priming. You start conversations with the right tone and framing. You plant seeds that move people. Confidence. Because you're training, not performing. This isn't theory anymore. It's muscle memory. Your brain builds new neural pathways by repetition. And every time you build a new pathway, it feels awkward. It feels like a struggle. It feels like you're doing something wrong. Instead, you're building that new pathway. According to some experts, it takes 63 to 64 repetitions to lay down that new neuropathway, that new behavioral groove, if you will. That's what the daily 15minute practice delivers.

This rewires your autopilot. Instead of panicking under pressure, you pause. Instead of blurting, you calibrate. Instead of pitching, you mirror. Instead of pushing, you label. And what's wild, when you do this, the other person feels heard, and they'll tell you everything you need to close a deal or repair the relationship. To recap, reps make instincts instincts win negotiations. 15 minutes a day rewires who you are in a room. Start today. Picked one label, one mirror, one calibrated question. Don't try to be great. Be consistent, which unleashes your greatness. Let's build the habit and the muscle that changes how the world listens to you. And remember, the person across the table is never the problem. The unsolved issue is. Let's solve it together. Be a black swan.

Steamroll (v.) - to forcefully dominate or overwhelm someone or something

例句: In negotiations, you shouldn’t let others **steamroll** your ideas.

Neural (adj.) - relating to the nerves or nervous system; concerning the brain’s learning pathways

例句: Negotiation is **neural**—repetition builds new brain pathways.

Tactical (adj.) - carefully planned to achieve a specific aim, often in a strategic context

例句: She practiced **tactical** empathy to respond effectively under pressure.

Empathy (n.) - the ability to understand and share the feelings of another

例句: Tactical **empathy** helps negotiators anticipate others’ reactions.

Calibrate (v.) - to adjust precisely for a particular function or outcome

例句: He **calibrated** his questions to suit the tone of the meeting.

Mirror (v.) - to imitate or reflect someone’s words, behavior, or tone to build rapport

例句: She **mirrored** the last few words of her colleague to show attentiveness.

Priming (n.) - preparing someone to respond in a desired way by setting context or tone beforehand

例句: **Priming** a conversation with the right framing influences its outcome.

Accusation (n.) - a statement claiming that someone has done something wrong or blameworthy

例句: He practiced the **accusations** audit to anticipate potential criticism.

Fluency (n.) - smoothness, skill, and ease in performing a task or using language

例句: Daily practice develops **fluency** in negotiation responses.

Instinct (n.) - an innate or developed natural tendency to act in a particular way

例句: With repetition, labeling threats becomes **instinct**.
