影片逐字稿 (Transcript)

Ready to transform your organizational skills from this, to this? What makes staying organized so important? Nearly 45% of employers say they like to see organizational ability on a resume when evaluating candidates. Staying organized is much easier said than done. In today's workshop, I'll show you the skills and simple systems you need in order to become a more organized person, and improve your overall effectiveness on the job.

Being organized sounds great, but what does it actually look like in practice? Being organized means managing your time, energy, and workspace in a way that enables you to deliver results consistently. It can look different depending on your job, but usually includes these ten skills.

Number one time management. You need strong time management to stay on top of projects and hit your deadlines. One way to strengthen this is by planning a quarterly schedule and anticipating workload peaks. This lets you see busy periods in advance and allocate your time more effectively.

Number two communication. Good communication keeps everyone aligned and prevents misunderstandings. An easy way to start cultivating good communication skills is to streamline your email and digital files, so that messages and information are easier to find and share.

Number three. Setting goals. Having clear goals gives your work direction and focus. Build this trait by conducting a brain dump at the end of each workweek to capture all pending tasks, then assigning each task a due date and adding it to a formal to do system.

Number four delegation. Delegation is about making sure the right work lands with the right person. You can make it easier by maintaining a consistent note taking system and workspace routine. So handoffs are clear and nothing gets lost in the shuffle.

Number five working under pressure. Pressure is inevitable at work, but being prepared makes it manageable to navigate. At the end of each day. Declutter your physical and digital workspaces to create a calmer starting point and quicker access to what you need when time is tight.

Number six. Self-motivation. Self-motivation keeps you moving without constant oversight. Support it by establishing workspace routines that help you stay on track without burning out. For example, you might start each morning by listing your top three priorities for the day, or set a timer to take short breaks so you stay focused and refreshed.

Number seven analytical thinking. Analytical thinking helps you break down the challenges and spot patterns, for example. Reviewing past performance data can help you anticipate workload peaks and give you the data you need to make smarter decisions.

Number eight attention to detail. Organized people are detail-oriented, which helps them make fewer errors and produce higher quality work as a result. Streamline your files and create clear labeling systems so nothing slips through the cracks.

Number nine decision making. When you're organized, you'll find it easier to make the right decisions faster. To do that, you'll want to have the right information always close at hand. Write down all possible outcomes, concerns, and possibilities related to the question at hand. Then reorder them by priority to focus your attention on the most important areas first. will help you visualize and vet all possible options to make the right call with confidence.

Number ten strategic planning. Strategic planning is about looking beyond today's to do list to ensure that daily actions align with the bigger picture. Integrate calendar planning across your digital and physical tools so you're always thinking ahead and aligning daily work with bigger goals.

When you're organized, workflows are smoother, deadlines are more manageable, and projects become much easier to track. Keeping everything in order this way not only boost your personal productivity, but also helps the entire business to run better. Plus, organization is the foundation for growth, from sharper critical thinking to clearer communication, and even increased the leadership opportunities. Being organized opens the door to higher level responsibilities and pay at work. Nailed it.

If there's one thing highly organized people love, it's a good list. Here at Indeed, we put together 78 of the best organizational tools available to help you improve your organizational skills. Here are the top seven we recommend you try. Screenshot this list. Check off the ones you already use and circle the ones you might want to try next, as much as you might want to. Don't skip the ones that sound totally out of your comfort zone. You might just find a new favorite.

Being organized isn't about trying to achieve perfection. If you spend all your time organizing, then you've missed the point. In the end, it's about incorporating skills that help you make it easier to do great work. Over time, these skills will give you the control, clarity, and calm you need to do the best work every day and feel great while doing it.
